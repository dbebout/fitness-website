LISS Workouts

Workout 1: The Incline Zone 2 Cruise (Treadmill)

This workout uses a steady incline instead of speed to elevate your heart rate, reducing high-impact stress on your knees and joints while burning fat.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Slow walking at 1.5% incline 5 minutes
Steady State Continuous walking — Incline 4.0%–6.0%, Speed 3.0–3.5 mph (breathe through your nose) 30–45 minutes
Cool-down 0% incline, drop speed by 0.5 mph each minute 3 minutes

Workout 2: The Progressive Distance Walk/Jog (Treadmill)

This option is best if you prefer flat ground. It introduces minimal speed variations just to keep your mind engaged without letting your heart rate spike into a HIIT zone.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Flat walking at 3.0 mph 5 minutes
Block 1 Brisk power walking at 3.5–3.8 mph (Incline 1.0%) 15 minutes
Block 2 Light easy jogging at 4.2–4.7 mph (Incline 1.0%) — drop to power walk if heart rate exceeds Zone 2 15 minutes
Block 3 Recovery power walk at 3.4 mph 10 minutes
Cool-down Slow walking 2 minutes

Workout 3: The Constant Cadence Flush (Peloton Bike)

This bike routine focuses on smooth, efficient pedaling mechanics. Keeping a high cadence with lower resistance flushes lactic acid out of your legs and builds aerobic endurance.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Easy pedaling, building cadence up to 80 RPM 5 minutes
Steady State Cadence 85–95 RPM, Resistance 25–35 (drop resistance by 2–3 if legs start burning) 40 minutes
Cool-down Slow spinning at less than 20 resistance, legs moving effortlessly 3 minutes
