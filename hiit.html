HIIT Workouts

Workout 1: The Speed & Incline Ladder (Treadmill)

This session focuses on building explosive leg power and high-end cardiovascular capacity by mixing speed jumps with progressive incline changes.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Brisk walking, transitioning into an easy jog 5 minutes
Interval 1 Sprint — flat 1% incline 45 seconds
Recovery 1 Slow walking 45 seconds
Interval 2 Fast run — 3% incline 45 seconds
Recovery 2 Slow walking 45 seconds
Interval 3 Fast run — 5% incline 45 seconds
Recovery 3 Slow walking 45 seconds
Repeat Perform the 3-step ladder a total of 4 times
Cool-down Slow walking 3 minutes

Workout 2: Pure Speed Intervals (Treadmill)

This workout targets maximum effort velocity. The goal is to hit your absolute top speed during the work windows, followed by complete rest to recover your breath.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Easy jogging 5 minutes
Work Window Max effort sprint (90%–100% of top speed) 30 seconds
Rest Window Walking or standing rest on side rails 60 seconds
Total Rounds Repeat work/rest cycle consecutively 10 rounds
Cool-down Slow walking 3 minutes

Workout 3: Tabata Power & Resistance (Peloton Bike)

This bike routine uses the traditional 20-second Tabata format combined with high resistance to build muscular endurance in your quads and glutes while spiking your heart rate.

Phase Details Duration
Warm-up Smooth pedaling, ramping cadence from 70 to 90 RPM 5 minutes
Block 1 — Speed Tabata Max effort sprint (Cadence 100+ RPM, moderate resistance) 20 seconds on / 10 seconds rest × 8
Block 1 Recovery Easy recovery ride 2 minutes
Block 2 — Climb Tabata Heavy standing climb (Resistance 55+, Cadence 60–70 RPM out of saddle) 20 seconds on / 10 seconds rest × 8
Cool-down Easy spinning, zero resistance 3 minutes
